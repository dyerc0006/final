Are you prepared for emergencies with your pet? If not, now's a great time to make your plans!

If you haven't already, make appointments with at least two emergency vets in your area to establish your pet with them. After, make sure your regular vet updates each emergency vet after every visit so they always have your pet's up to date vaccinations and exam history. This cuts down on check-in time, and gives the emergency staff a complete history of your pet.

Next, get together an emergency bag of everything your pet will need in the event of a fire, tornado, or other emergency event. This should be put in an easily accessible place and contain:

  • any medications the pet may need
  • collapsible food and water bowls
  • bottled water
  • enough food for 1 to 2 weeks
  • leash and collar/harness
  • cat litter and pan
  • blankets
  • basic pet first-aid kit
  • flashlight with extra batteries
  • copy of medical history and vaccines
  • emergency contacts
  • a favorite toy
